    University News

    $3 Million NSF HSI Grant to Help Improve Undergraduate STEM Education

    The university has received a $3 million award from the National Science Foundation (NSF) for a program designed to expand research and career-readiness opportunities for undergraduate STEM students.

    CPP No. 1 Among Polytechnics for Degrees to Hispanic Students

    Hispanic Outlook on Education ranks Cal Poly Pomona No. 1 among polytechnic universities and No. 18 among all U.S. universities for the number of Hispanic students graduating with Bachelor degrees in 2019-20.

    Fairplex Donates Vast Archive to Cal Poly Pomona

    In November, CPP announced that Fairplex had donated its extensive collection of historical records to the University Library Special Collections and Archives.

    A Transformative Gift

    In a message to the Cal Poly Pomona community, Pres. Soraya Coley announced a $40 million gift to the university from philanthropist and author MacKenzie Scott. The largest gift ever received from an individual, these life-changing funds will empower Cal Poly Pomona's students success and institutional excellence efforts in perpetuity.

    Teaching Virtually and Remotely

    In partnership with the Center for the Advancement of Faculty Excellence (CAFE), colleges and departments are supporting faculty to teach virtually and/or remotely. The CAFE Website has resources and quick start guides for online tools like Blackboard, Zoom and Kaltura.

    Online Engagement and Support
