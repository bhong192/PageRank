Administration | Financial Operations | Research Programs

Administration | Financial Operations | Research Programs (Ops) is the central coordinating point for the Office of Research (OR). Having similar functionality to an academic school’s dean’s office, it is responsible for all business and financial operations of the entire Office of Research (OR) organization.  This includes fiduciary responsibility for the coordinating point’s budget and assigned square footage of space across the campus and at the University Research Park.  OR consists of four units that are internal to the organization.  These units are the Vice Chancellor’s Office, Administration | Financial Operations | Research Programs, Research Administration, Research Development and University Laboratory Animals Resources (ULAR).  In addition, Ops oversees the administration and business functions of units that are considered external as they have an affiliation with academic schools on campus. These units are multi-disciplinary and interdisciplinary research units that report to the Vice Chancellor for Research.

The business functions that Ops is responsible for incorporate strategic planning, long range and short term planning, management, oversight and administrative support at both a micro and macro level in the areas of financial management, budgeting, business processes, human resources, academic personnel, procurement services, space and facilities management for all units under the jurisdiction of OR.

      Sinqui Musto is currently the Associate Vice Chancellor, Administration/Financial Operations/Research Programs in the Office of Research at the University of California, Irvine.       Karen Tano
      • Personnel Manager

      Karen Tano serves as the Personnel Manager for the Office of Research at the University of California, Irvine.  In  her role, she handles all areas related to academic and staff payroll/personnel for the Office of Research (OR) Coordinating Point, which includes the internal units such as the Vice Chancellor for Research, Sponsored Projects Administration,  Research Protections,  Electronic Research Administration and the University Laboratory Animal Resources as well as the over 25 research units that report through OR.  Karen serves as a key resource and acts as a consultant regarding personnel matters and provides expertise for interpreting and applying appropriate university policy and procedures as it relates to the matter at hand.  She serves as the Department Benefits Representative with responsibility for on-boarding, as well as other benefit areas including, but not limited to, leave management, worker’s compensation, and terminations.  In addition, Karen acts as a consultant to the Organized Research Unit Administrators and Directors on issues and serves as the liaison with the Office of Academic Personnel on all academic matters.

      Karen is a member of the Campus Personnel Officers group, which was formed to enable the collaboration with other high level personnel employees in the Schools in order to foster the development and the sharing of best practices in both human resources and  academic personnel.  This provides her with additional tools and  knowledge in order for her to be a better resource to her colleagues in the OR coordinating point. 

      Karen earned her B.A. degree in Organizational Communication from Pepperdine University and has worked at the University for nearly 20 years.
