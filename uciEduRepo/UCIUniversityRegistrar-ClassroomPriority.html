Abbreviations and Notes


Room Type Abbreviations

1. Lec: Lecture Hall (fixed seating or theater-style seating - capacities listed do not reflect spaces for wheelchairs - BS3 1200, DBH 1100, DBH 1600, EH 1200, ELH 100, HG 1800, HIB 100, HSLH 100A, MSTB 120, MSTB 122, MSTB 124, PCB 1100, PSLH 100, RH 101, RH 104, SE2 1304, SH 128, SH 174, SSH 100, SSL 140, SSL 228, SSL 248, SSL 270, SSL 290, SSLH 100, and SSPA 1100 all have extra spaces for wheelchairs)

2. ALC: Active Learning Classroom (7 to 14 work group 'pods' with movable seating and advanced multiple displays for group work instruction)

3. Case Seminar Room (fixed, tiered-level seating with long tables)

4. Cls: Classroom (moveable, tablet arm chairs)

5. Sem: Seminar (tables and chairs)

6. Trl: Trailer Classroom ("portable", modular classrooms with SmartClassroom features - see CTS website for more details)


Media Abbreviations

1. LTS: Limited Technical Support - technical support only available between 8-5pm Monday through Friday

2. NM: No media of any type recommended for this room

3. SMRT: SmartClassrooms - renovated GACs equipped with instructional technology (visit CTS website or contact smartclassrooms@uci.edu for more information)

General Scheduling Policies

1. Departments are instructed to schedule classes on-module in their first-priority General Assignment Classroom space BEFORE requesting the use of other rooms on this list.

2. Normally, 3 and 4 unit courses must be scheduled for three 50-minute periods per week (as Monday-Wednesday-Friday) beginning on the hour from 8:00 a.m. to 6:50 pm or two 80-minute periods per week (as Tuesday-Thursday) from 8:00 a.m. to 6:20 pm Normally, 5 unit courses are scheduled daily for 50-minute periods per week (as Monday-Tuesday-Wednesday-Thursday-Friday) beginning on the hour from 8:00 a.m. to 6:50 pm. Buildings are closed by 10pm Monday through Friday.

3. Specialized facility needs, such as laboratory demonstrations and unique media services, will be considered and accommodated whenever possible. However, maximum room utilization must take precedence.

4. Requests which under-utilize space will be scheduled at less popular hours after other scheduling needs have been met.

5. Three-hour courses will be scheduled after multi-day courses. One-hour discussions (zero-unit classes) have the lowest priority and are scheduled last.

6. Room requests must be made on the basis of anticipated enrollments being equal to or greater than 80 percent of the room capacity. Courses must show significant enrollment history at the capacity requested. The University Registrar will assign rooms based on this principle of maximum room utilization.
